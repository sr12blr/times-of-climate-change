Badhai Ho! A Great Indian Bustard Is Born

First chick hatched in a decade signals India's conservation efforts are working

What's happening?

  • A chick of the critically endangered Great Indian Bustard hatched in Gujarat—the first one born in the wild in over a decade
  • The bird was part of conservation efforts by the Indian government, with successful breeding in natural habitat
  • The Great Indian Bustard is one of India's rarest birds, with fewer than 150 individuals left in the wild

Why should you care?

  • This is proof that India's conservation efforts actually work when given time and resources
  • Bustards are a barometer of grassland health—their survival means the ecosystems they depend on are recovering
  • India has a moral responsibility to preserve its endemic species, and this shows we can do it
