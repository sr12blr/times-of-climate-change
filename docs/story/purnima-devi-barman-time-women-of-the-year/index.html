Purnima Devi Barman Named in TIME's 2025 Women of the Year

Dr. Purnima Devi Barman received recognition from TIME magazine for conserving the greater adjutant stork in Assam, lifting the species from endangered to near-threatened status.

Biodiversity

What are we talking about?

  • Dr. Purnima Devi Barman received recognition from TIME magazine for conserving the greater adjutant stork in Assam, lifting the species from endangered to near-threatened status.
  • Her 'Hargila Army' of 20,000+ women protects nesting sites and educates communities, blending conservation with women's economic empowerment.

Why should you care?

  • Her work demonstrates how local action sparks global change while protecting vulnerable populations.
  • It's a rare story of a conservation effort that is led by women, empowers women economically, and has measurably improved the status of an endangered species.
