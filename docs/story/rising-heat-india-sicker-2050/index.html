Too Hot to Move: How Rising Heat Could Make India Sicker by 2050

A study published in The Lancet Planetary Health (based on data from 1.9 mn adults across 146 countries) found that rising temperatures worldwide are making people less physically active — and this is likely to get worse.

Heat Health Mobility

What are we talking about?

  • A study published in The Lancet Planetary Health (based on data from 1.9 mn adults across 146 countries) found that rising temperatures worldwide are making people less physically active — and this is likely to get worse.
  • South Asia, including India, is among the worst-hit regions because outdoor activity becomes dangerous when heat and humidity climb together.

Why should you care?

  • India already has a growing diabetes and heart disease epidemic. If people move even less because it's too hot to go outside, these numbers will spike further.
  • Daily wage workers, farmers, and outdoor vendors — tens of millions of Indians — can't just stay indoors. They face the double burden of heat exposure and declining health.
  • Cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Chennai already see summer temperatures that make outdoor exercise risky. This study suggests that window of good weather that allows outdoor exercise is shrinking every year.

What can we do about it?

  • The findings signal that India needs to plan for indoor public spaces, covered walkways, and cooled community areas — not just as comfort, but as public health infrastructure.
