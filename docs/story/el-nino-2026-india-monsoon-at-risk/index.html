El Nino is likely in 2026: India's monsoon is at risk.

Scientists are predicting a 62% chance of El Nino developing by August 2026.

Heat Water Food Health

What are we talking about?

  • Scientists are predicting a 62% chance of El Nino developing by August 2026.
  • El Nino is a cyclical global weather condition that results in a weaker monsoon and more intense summer heat in India.

Why should you care?

  • El Nino typically weakens India's southwest monsoon — the single biggest source of water for the country's farms, reservoirs, and groundwater. This comes right after an unusually dry winter across northern India. Groundwater hasn't recharged, reservoirs are low, and glacial snowpack is thin. If the monsoon also under delivers, India could be heading into a serious water crunch by late 2026.
  • A weak monsoon means less water for kharif crops like rice, soybean, and pulses, and that means higher food prices by the end of the year.
  • El Nino years also bring more intense and longer heat waves. India is already seeing temperatures cross 40°C in March. If El Nino strengthens through summer, the heat could be brutal.
