The World Is Water-Bankrupt. India Is One of the Worst Hit.

Calling it a 'water crisis' is not cutting it any more.

Water Food

What are we talking about?

  • Calling it a 'water crisis' is not cutting it any more.
  • In January, scientists officially declared the world has entered 'global water bankruptcy' — meaning we're withdrawing more water than nature can replenish, and some of that damage is now permanent.
  • India, with 18% of the world's people but only 4% of its freshwater, is deep in this overdraft.

Why should you care?

  • Around 600 million Indians face high to extreme water stress. Per capita water availability has fallen to about 1,100 cubic metres — below the international water stress threshold.
  • Chennai, Bengaluru, and Delhi have all faced acute shortages in recent years, not because of one bad monsoon, but because demand has structurally outgrown supply. Most cities are extracting ground water at a rate that far exceeds the rate of replenishment.
  • Today is World Water Day, and this year's theme is water and gender, to bring attention to how water scarcity and gender are closely linked. Women and girls globally spend 250 million hours a day collecting water — three times more than men. In rural India, women still walk kilometres in the heat for a single bucket.
