India is the 6th most polluted country globally (2025 World Air Quality Report)

Persistent coughs, higher bills, kids stuck at home — air pollution is already part of your daily life

What's happening?

IQAir’s 2025 World Air Quality Report is out — India is the 6th most polluted country in the world. This is not new, it’s been in the top 10 every single year since IQAir started tracking.

Loni in Ghaziabad, UP topped the list as the most polluted city on the planet, with PM2.5 levels at 112.5 — nearly 20 times the WHO safe limit. Four more Indian cities made the global top 10: Byrnihat (Meghalaya), Delhi, Ghaziabad (UP), and Ula/Birnagar (West Bengal). Delhi has now been the world’s most polluted capital for 8 years running.

The silver lining? India’s average PM2.5 has been slowly dropping — from 54.4 in 2023 to 48.9 in 2025. But that’s still nearly 10x what the WHO considers safe.

Why should you care?

This is not a distant issue. The severe levels of air pollution are already affecting your everyday life.

Your health: persistent coughs, fatigue, headaches, weaker lungs — even if you feel “fine.”

Your money: more spending on healthcare, purifiers, electricity.

Your family: kids and elderly face higher risks from the same air.

Your lifestyle: fewer walks, worse winters, more time indoors.
