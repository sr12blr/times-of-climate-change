Two Kilos of Soil, Rs 68 Lakh: The Maharashtra Village That Struck Biodiversity Gold

What are we talking about?

  • Dapur is a village near Nashik with a population of ~8,000.
  • In 2024, a company ‘Advanced Enzyme Technologies Ltd’ collected soil from the village while searching for microorganisms. They found a bacterium useful for probiotics, from which they made 42 products for commercial use.
  • Under India’s biodiversity laws, companies that use biological resources sourced from local ecosystems must share part of the benefits with the communities connected to those resources.
  • And so the company shared back part of the profits — 68 lakhs with the Gram Panchayat.

Why should you care?

  • Rare good-news story: village actually got paid for what was taken from its land. It’s a rare example of biodiversity actually getting valued.
  • India’s biodiversity law requires profit-sharing, but most villages don’t know it exists.
  • This could be a way to incentivise communities to adopt better conservation practices, natural farming etc.
