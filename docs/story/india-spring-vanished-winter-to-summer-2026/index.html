The Era of Climate 'Weirding': India Jumps Straight from Winter to Summer

What are we talking about?

  • Instead of spring, summer arrived overnight. Mumbai was already at 40°C and Shimla 25°C in March. And then days later, hailstorms.

Why should you care?

  • Food prices may go up: Wheat needs cool weather before harvest. This year’s heat hit Punjab and Haryana’s crop at exactly that stage, and farmers are predicting a weaker harvest.
  • Water could be next: there’s an 80% chance of El Niño this year, which usually means a weaker monsoon. Dry winter + weak monsoon = water shortages.
  • The seasons are becoming unpredictable. When one month gives you 40°C and hailstorms, it’s not just farmers who can’t plan — it’s all of us.
